Parts of Speech

What is Parts of Speech in English?

Parts of Speech is the words that we use in either speaking or writing/typing. They are the words that makes us express, speak, and relay our feelings in multiple ways. There are 8 parts; noun, pronoun, verb, adjective, adverb, preposition, conjunction, and interjection. Let's talk about them!

The 8 Parts

Noun

/noun/
It is a place, person, or thing.
It is usually the subject of the sentence. There are 2 nouns; the proper noun and the common noun. A proper noun is capitalized and is a name/specific place, person, or thing. Like Baguio, James, or Gucci . A common noun on the other hand, is not capitalized and is a none-specific place, person, or thing. Like a park, a person, or car .
Example: Baguio is the summer capital.
Example: Olive is a good teacher.
Example: I am going to the church .
Example: Is that your house ?

Pronoun

/ˈprōˌnoun/
It is a replacement/substitute for a noun and usually refers to a person or thing.
In a pronoun, it would always substitute a noun like James to he/him, or Carla to she/her. The original nouns are called antecedent.
Example: Is she ok with coming out?
Example: They are good people.
Example: It doesn't work anymore.

Verb

/vərb/
It is a action, state, or occurence.
It is usually located in the predicate part of a sentence. There are the action verbs, linking verbs, and auxillary verbs (helping verbs). Action verbs is when a person does something; an action. Like attack or read . Linking verbs are verbs that connects the subject to a adjective. Like is or are . Auxillary verbs adds purpose/function to a clause. Like must or would .
Example: I am going to sing .
Example: Charles is gonna give us chocolate.
Example: I will be brought back later.

Adjective

/vərb/
It describes something or someone.
It is usually located in the both subject and predicate part. It usually answers the question of which one, what kind, or how many. Like charming or perfect .
Example: You look very beautiful today!
Example: A few people are naturally immune to the virus.

Adverb

/ˈajəktiv/
It modifies a verb, another adverb, adjective, or a sentence.
It is usually located at the predicate part. It cannot modify a noun or a pronoun. Adverbs are softly or loudly .
Example: He ran quickly today.
Example: James happily asked us if we are free today.

Preposition

/ˌprepəˈziSH(ə)n/
A preposition is a word placed before a noun or pronoun to form a phrase modifying another word in the sentence.
Some examples are at or in .
Example: I am inbetween building A and B.
Example: Besides , it's still early for me.

Conjuction

/kənˈjəNG(k)SH(ə)n/
It connects 2 or more sentences, clauses, or words.
It connects 2 parts combining them, making a new sentence. Like but or if .
Example: We are going to John Hay and Burnham .
Example: Which do you like, ice cream or candy ?

Interjection

/ˌin(t)ərˈjekSH(ə)n/
It is usually a burst of emotion and is not accompanied by other words.
It is a phrase where you would express either pain, pleasure, displeasure, suprise, or fear. It is also usually exclamatory. Like Ow! or Boo! . Example: Eek! What is that?!
Example: Shoot! They're here!

What did we learn in a nutshell?

English is a complicated, but a beautiful language that is spoken everywhere. It is the reason why we can communicate and understand each other. It made a lot of people's lives and is the reason why we are still here today.
