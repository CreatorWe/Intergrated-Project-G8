Volleyball

Volleyball! Quite famous here in the Philippines and the rest for the world. Invented by William G. Morgan in 1895, it was orginally designed to be an indoor sport for businessmen (a variation of basketball). The sport grew and grew in the US and was eventually introduced to the rest of the world. As the sport grew, it became more competitive with the first international competition in 1913, held in Manila, and it being an official olympic sport at the 1964 Olympic Games held in Tokyo. And here we are today, the sport is popular as it can ever be and has changed the sport world.

Rules and How to Play

In Volleyball, there is a rectangular court with a net in the middle seperating each side with 2 teams, comprising of 6 people per team on either side. Your goal is to hit the ball to touch the oppenents side of the court to get a point. If the ball is outside of the court, both sides won't get a point. There are 5 sets with 25 points per set (If they are too close in points, they are allowed to go over 25 points). To win you are to win atleast 3 out of the 5 sets. You are not allowed to touch the net, block a serve, hitting the ball more than 3 times, intentional physical contact (punching, kicking, etc.) going over the net/line, etc.

Ways of Hitting the Ball

The are multiple ways of hitting the ball. There are serving, which is done at the start of a round and is not in the usual boundaries of the court (initiates the round), receiving, it is where you would receive the ball and pass it to a teammate for a set, toss, or spike, digging, which is where you would receive and pass a ball which was so close to the ground, setting, which is where you would set the ball for a spike, spiking, which is where you would hit the ball unto the other side via jumping, and tossing, which is where you would hit the ball unto the other side like the way you would receive a ball.

Positions

In Volleyball, there are six positions. The Right Hitter, Middle Blocker, Middle Hitter, Left Hitter, and Libero. The middle blocker and middle hitter (or libero) are in the middle (the middle blocker being the front and middle hitter [or libero] at the back), while there is 4 left and right hitters in the front and back. All of them can set and score (with the libero being very limited). The libero would only appear when their team is receiving, and would not appear when serving (you would serve if you get a point and receive if the oppenent gets a point). Positions would also rotate if there is a new team serving. The front row players (right and left hitter, middle blocker) are the ones who are gonna block, set, receive, and score. The back row players (right, left, and middle hitter [or libero]) are the ones who are gonna set, receive, and score. The front and back row are allowed to go and move around in their own respective areas, but the back row players are not allowed to cross the 10 ft line while the front row can. The libero is a defensive role and is the one who usually specializes in ball control and defense.

Basketball

basketball! The 3rd most famous sport in the entire world! Invented by James Naismith on 1891, in YMCA Training School, US, It grew steadily and slowly getting popular in the us and eventually the world. The very first international match was in 1909 held in Saint Petersburg. The sport was even in the 1936 Olympics held in Berlin as an official sport. Then in the 1980s with cable telivision being a thing, it exploded in popularity and later on became a staple in sports and US culture.

Rules and How to Play

In basketball, there is a rectangular court with a hoop on the opposing sides with 2 teams, having 5 people per team. Your goal is to get the ball into the opposing side's hoop to get a point(s). If the ball gets outside of the court, the ball is thrown back into the court by the one who threw it (can be passed to a teammate in this moment), if they do not throw the ball in 5 seconds, the ball goes to the enemy. There are 4 quarters with 12 minutes per quarter (48 min). After 2 quarters, the teams switches sides and takes a 15 minute break. To win, you are to get more points than your opponent until the end of the time limit. If it is a tie, an overtime can happen until one side has more points. You are not allowed to travel (holding the ball while running without dribbling), grabbing, hitting, tripping, etc.

Ball Handling

In basketball, there are multiple ways to handle the ball. The first is dribbling, it is where you would bounce the ball from your hand to the ground repeatedly, you are not allowed to stop dribbling, but if you do, it is when you are going to pass the ball or shoot it. Second is passing, it is when you are to throw your ball to another teammate. Third is shooting, this is when you would shoot the ball into the hoop from a close to far distance. Depending on where/from you are shooting it, you would earn 1-3 points from doing this. Fourth is dunking, it is where you would jump and slam into the hoop, getting the ball to go through it. You would earn 2 points from doing this.

Positions

In basketball, there are 5 positions: Center, which is the one that tries to score in close shots or rebounds, but on defense, he is the one who blocks the opponents shots and rebound their misses. Power Forward, which is kind of the same as centers, but takes longer shots. They also roam all over the court. Small Forward, which is the one who scores points and is very defensive, they are usually also the secondary to a Power Forward or Center. Point Guard, which is the one who is usually on the offense and is the one that usually would steal the ball. Shooting Guard, which is the primary shooter and would be the one who would shoot the ball into the hoop.
